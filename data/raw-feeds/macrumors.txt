		<title>Apple&#039;s Low-Cost Colorful MacBook: All the Rumors</title>
		<link>https://www.macrumors.com/2026/02/20/low-cost-macbook-latest-rumors/</link>
		<comments>https://forums.macrumors.com/threads/apples-low-cost-colorful-macbook-all-the-rumors.2478001/</comments>
		<pubDate>Fri, 20 Feb 2026 14:37:39 PST</pubDate>
		<guid isPermaLink="false">https://www.macrumors.com/2026/02/20/low-cost-macbook-latest-rumors/</guid>
		<dc:creator>Juli Clover</dc:creator>
		<category>Featured</category>
		

		<description><![CDATA[Apple has been developing a more affordable version of the MacBook, and it's rumored to be launching in under two weeks. This is going to be one of Apple's most unique Macs, because there hasn't really been anything quite like it before.<br/>
<br/>
<img src="https://images.macrumors.com/article-new/2025/12/Low-Cost-A18-Pro-MacBook-Feature-Pink.jpg" alt="" width="2500" height="1406" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-1028724" /><br/>
We've rounded up everything we know about the low-cost MacBook ahead of its March debut.<br/>
<br/>
<h2>Design</h2><br/>
Rumors about the MacBook's design make it sound a lot like the <a href="https://www.macrumors.com/roundup/macbook-air/">MacBook Air</a>. It will have an aluminum chassis in various colors, and a 12.9-inch or 13-inch display, depending on the rumor.<br/>
<br/>
<img src="https://images.macrumors.com/article-new/2026/02/Multicolored-Low-Cost-A18-Pro-MacBook-Feature.jpg" alt="" width="2500" height="1406" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-1036750" /><br/>
It's possible the low-cost MacBook will have a thin and light design because it's going to use a lower power A-series chip that doesn't require a lot of heat dissipation, but that's not yet confirmed. Apple used to have a 12-inch MacBook with a thin design and a low-power Core M chip, and it's been suggested that this new MacBook could be something of a revival of that machine.<br/>
<br/>
Thinner and lighter typically means more expensive with Apple products, so a super slim design might not be what Apple is optimizing for. Making the low-cost MacBook thinner than the &zwnj;MacBook Air&zwnj; could just confuse the MacBook lineup.<br/>
<br/>
With the low-cost <a href="https://www.macrumors.com/roundup/ipad/">iPad</a>, Apple keeps the price down by using older display technology that's not as thin, so we could see that same strategy with the low-cost MacBook. A thicker chassis and a super efficient chip could mean a long battery life, which would be ideal for an educational environment.<br/>
<br/>
<h2>Colors</h2><br/>
The MacBook is going to come in a <a href="https://www.macrumors.com/2026/02/16/low-cost-macbook-yellow-green-blue-pink/">selection of fun colors</a>, and Apple has tested light yellow, light green, blue, pink, silver, and dark gray, according to <em>Bloomberg</em>. Not all of those colors are likely to ship, but it sounds like we'll get at least four of them.<br/>
<br/>
Apple analyst <a href="https://www.macrumors.com/guide/ming-chi-kuo/">Ming-Chi Kuo</a> thinks the MacBook will come in yellow, silver, blue, and pink, which would be the same colors that Apple offers for the &zwnj;iPad&zwnj;.<br/>
<br/>
<h2>A-Series Chip</h2><br/>
The most consistent rumor we've heard about the MacBook is its planned chip. Rather than an M-series Mac chip, Apple is planning to use an A-series chip. The low-cost MacBook is expected to use the A18 Pro chip, which Apple first debuted in the <a href="https://www.macrumors.com/roundup/iphone-16/">iPhone 16</a> Pro.<br/>
<br/>
<img src="https://images.macrumors.com/article-new/2024/09/A18-Pro-Chip.jpg" alt="" width="1600" height="900" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-965379" /><br/>
The A18 Pro uses a second-generation 3-nanometer process. It has a 6-core CPU with four performance cores and two efficiency cores, along with a 6-core GPU and a 16-core Neural Engine for AI-based tasks. In Geekbench benchmarks, the A18 Pro has an average single-core score of 3451, and a multi-core score of 8572. For comparison, the M4 <a href="https://www.macrumors.com/roundup/ipad-pro/">iPad Pro</a> earns a single-core score of 3694 and a multi-core score of 13732 (Apple's next &zwnj;MacBook Air&zwnj; is going to use the M4 chip).<br/>
<br/>
The A18 Pro outperforms the <a href="https://www.macrumors.com/guide/m1/">M1</a>, which is the chip that Apple kept around in a lower-cost version of the &zwnj;MacBook Air&zwnj; for several years. An A18 MacBook wouldn't be too far off from the M4 Mac/&zwnj;iPad&zwnj; chips in terms of single-core performance, but there would be a difference in multi-core performance.<br/>
<br/>
A MacBook with the A18 chip would be more than powerful enough for day-to-day use like web browsing, document creation, watching videos, and even light photo and video editing. 		<title>Apple Watch: 15 Tips Every Owner Needs to Know</title>
		<link>https://www.macrumors.com/2026/02/19/apple-watch-15-tips-need-to-know/</link>
		<comments>https://forums.macrumors.com/threads/apple-watch-15-tips-every-owner-needs-to-know.2477935/</comments>
		<pubDate>Thu, 19 Feb 2026 07:38:03 PST</pubDate>
		<guid isPermaLink="false">https://www.macrumors.com/2026/02/19/apple-watch-15-tips-need-to-know/</guid>
		<dc:creator>Tim Hardwick</dc:creator>
		<category>Featured</category>
		

		<description><![CDATA[Apple Watch is now eleven generations in, and packed with useful features that are easy to miss at first glance. To help you get more out of your new device, we've rounded up 15 practical tips you might not have discovered yet, including a few that long-time users often overlook.<br/>
<br/>
<img src="https://images.macrumors.com/article-new/2026/02/Apple-Watch-15-Tips-Every-Owner-Needs-to-Know-Feature.jpg" alt="" width="2500" height="1406" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-1036634" /><br/>
<br/>
<h2>Bounce Between Two Apps</h2><br/>
<img src="https://images.macrumors.com/article-new/2026/02/apple-watch-app-switcher.jpeg" alt="watch" width="1920" height="1080" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-1036616" /><br/>
On your Apple Watch, double-press the Digital Crown to see a deck of all currently open apps, and turn it to scroll through them. From this view, you can jump back to the last app you were using. Simply tap on an app screen to switch to it, or swipe left on its card and tap the red X button to quit it.<br/>
<br/>
<h2>Switch App Views</h2><br/>
<img src="https://images.macrumors.com/article-new/2026/02/apple-watch-apps-view.jpeg" alt="app view" width="1920" height="1080" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-1036618" /><br/>
If the app grid feels messy, switch to List View. Open the Watch app on iPhone, tap App View, and choose List View. From then on, pressing the Digital Crown will show your apps in a simple, scrollable list.<br/>
<br/>
<h2>Rearrange Apps</h2><br/>
<img src="https://images.macrumors.com/article-new/2026/02/watch-app-iphone-app-view.jpeg" alt="watch app" width="1920" height="1080" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-1036626" /><br/>
You can rearrange your apps so that the ones you use most are closer to hand. Simply press and hold on any app in the grid view, then drag it where you want. Alternatively, open the Watch app on your iPhone, go to App View ➝ Arrangement, and move things around there instead.<br/>
<br/>
<h2>Ping Your iPhone</h2><br/>
<img src="https://images.macrumors.com/article-new/2026/02/apple-watch-ping.jpeg" alt="ping" width="1920" height="1080" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-1036617" /><br/>
If you've misplaced your iPhone but you're sure it's nearby, open Control Center with a press of the Side button, then tap the phone icon to make it ping. Press and hold that icon and the iPhone's camera flash will blink too, which can help if it's hidden under something.<br/>
<br/>
<h2>Skip the Countdown</h2><br/>
<img src="https://images.macrumors.com/article-new/2026/02/apple-watch-workout-countdown.jpeg" alt="workout" width="1920" height="1080" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-1036619" /><br/>
If you're eager to start a workout, the three-second countdown before it starts can be skipped. Just tap the screen when the countdown begins and your workout will start immediately. If you find yourself doing this regularly, consider turning on Precision Start in Settings ➝ Workout.<br/>
<br/>
<h2>Customize Vibration Strength</h2><br/>
<img src="https://images.macrumors.com/article-new/2026/02/prominent-haptics-apple-watch.jpeg" alt="haptics" width="1920" height="1080" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-1036897" /><br/>
If you keep missing notifications, go to Settings ➝ Sounds & Haptics ➝ and change from Default to Prominent. This adds an extra tap pattern before alerts so they're harder to ignore.<br/>
<br/>
<h2>Perform Precision Timing</h2><br/>
<img src="https://images.macrumors.com/article-new/2026/02/chronograph-pro-watch-face.jpeg" alt="chronograph" width="1920" height="1080" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-1036898" /><br/>
The Chronograph Pro watch face transforms into an actual chronograph. Tap the outer edge surrounding the main 12-hour dial on this watch face to record time on scales of 60, 30, 6, or 3 seconds. Alternatively, select the tachymeter timescale to measure speed based on time travel over a fixed distance.<br/>
<br/>
<h2>Jump to the Top</h2><br/>
<img src="https://images.macrumors.com/article-new/2026/02/apple-watch-jump-to-top.jpeg" alt="jump to top" width="1920" height="1080" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-1036621" /><br/>
If you've scrolled way down in an app and want to jump back to the top, just tap the time in the top corner of the screen. It works in most apps and saves a lot of scrolling.<br/>
<br/>
<h2>Remove Apps</h2><br/>
<img src="https://images.macrumors.com/article-new/2026/02/uninstall-apple-watch-apps.jpeg" alt="apps view" width="1920" height="1080" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-1036622" /><br/>
Clearing out apps you don't use on your Apple Watch is easy. In the List or Grid View, press and hold on the screen until the apps jiggle, then tap the small x in the corner of the app icon to delete it. This works for most system apps and all third-party apps.<br/>
<br/>
<h2>Customize Control Center</h2><br/>
<img src="https://images.macrumors.com/article-new/2026/02/apple-watch-customize-control-center.jpeg" alt="control center" width="1920" height="1080" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-1036623" /><br/>
By default, Control Center (accessed via the Side button) gives you quick access to things like Wi-Fi, battery, and Do Not Disturb. But it's worth seeing what else you can add to it that you'd like quick access to. Tap the Edit button at the bottom, then tap the + icon in the top-left corner of the screen. System options such as New Note and Lights are particularly handy, and you might see some third-party options listed too, depending on your installed apps.<br/>
<br/>
<h2>Speak the Time Out Loud</h2><br/>
<img src="https://images.macrumors.com/article-new/2026/02/apple-watch-mickey-mouse.jpeg" alt="mickey" width="1920" height="1080" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-1036624" /><br/>
If you're using the Mickey or Minnie watch face, tap on the character and they'll speak the time out loud. Just make sure your sound is turned on. In fact, you can also have Siri read the time on any watch face by tapping and holding with two fingers on the display. Again though, sound needs to be enabled.<br/>
<br/>
<h2>Customize Smart Replies</h2><br/>
<img src="https://images.macrumors.com/article-new/2026/02/watch-app-smart-replies.jpeg" alt="smart replies" width="1920" height="1080" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-1036627" /><br/>
Smart Replies are handy when you want to reply with just a few words. In the Watch app on iPhone, go to Messages and tap Default Replies to customise what shows up. Then when a message comes in, simply swipe down to pick one of your preset replies.<br/>
<br/>
<h2>Pause Activity Rings</h2><br/>
<img src="https://images.macrumors.com/article-new/2026/02/pause-apple-watch-activity-rings.jpeg" alt="activity" width="1920" height="1080" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-1036901" /><br/>
Feeling unwell but hoping to keep your streak intact? In the Activity app, select your rings to access the option to suspend them for the day, or set a pause that lasts until a chosen date up to 90 days ahead.<br/>
<br/>
<h2>Create a Note</h2><br/>
<img src="https://images.macrumors.com/article-new/2026/02/create-note-watch.jpeg" alt="notes" width="1920" height="1080" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-1036904" /><br/>
In the new Notes app in watchOS 26, you can't modify existing notes on Apple Watch, but you can create a new one by tapping the compose control in the bottom-right corner and speaking your text. Because Notes sync through iCloud, you can refine or reorganize everything later on a device with a physical or on-screen keyboard.<br/>
<br/>
<h2>Mute and Dismiss Alerts</h2><br/>
<img src="https://images.macrumors.com/article-new/2025/07/wrist-flick-watchos-26.gif" alt="" width="1920" height="1080" class="aligncenter size-full wp-image-1011088" /><br/>
With a quick wrist flip, you can clear the current screen and go back to the watch face. The same gesture can be used to mute calls, stop timers, and dismiss notifications. The feature, which is on by default, is supported on Apple Watch SE (3rd generation), Series 9, Ultra 2, and later.<br/>This article, &quot;<a href="https://www.macrumors.com/2026/02/19/apple-watch-15-tips-need-to-know/">Apple Watch: 15 Tips Every Owner Needs to Know</a>&quot; first appeared on <a href="https://www.macrumors.com">MacRumors.com</a><br/><br/><a href="https://forums.macrumors.com/threads/apple-watch-15-tips-every-owner-needs-to-know.2477935/">Discuss this article</a> in our forums<br/><br/>]]></description>

		<description><![CDATA[A reported hardware failure affecting Apple's new in-house C1X 5G modem in the <a href="https://www.macrumors.com/roundup/iphone-air/">iPhone Air</a> has surfaced online, marking the first known real-world incident involving the company's own baseband technology.<br/>
<br/>
<img src="https://images.macrumors.com/article-new/2021/03/Apple-5G-Modem-Feature-16x9.jpg" alt="" width="2240" height="1260" class="alignnone size-full wp-image-788396" /><br/>
The &zwnj;iPhone Air&zwnj; is the first <a href="https://www.macrumors.com/guide/iphone/">iPhone</a> model to ship with the Apple's internally designed C1X 5G modem, replacing Qualcomm's X75 modem used across the <a href="https://www.macrumors.com/roundup/iphone-16/">iPhone 16</a> lineup. The transition to Apple-designed modems follows years of development after Apple acquired Intel's smartphone modem business in 2019 and began building its own baseband engineering teams and intellectual property portfolio with the goal of reducing reliance on Qualcomm and increasing efficiency.<br/>
<br/>
A newly reported incident was first documented in a <a href="https://www.reddit.com/r/iphone/comments/1r8bmth/so_the_iphone_air_c1x_modem_died_suddenly/">support thread</a> on Reddit spotted by <em><a href="https://wccftech.com/iphone-air-c1x-5g-modem-failure-spotted-online/">Wccftech</a></em>, where the user "itstheskylion" described waking up to find the device had completely lost cellular reception. According to the user, the phone displayed no signal bars and diagnostics indicated a hardware-level cellular problem.<br/>
<br/>
The device had reportedly been kept in a case since purchase and showed no signs of physical damage. The user said that multiple troubleshooting steps were attempted, including restarting the device, performing a soft reset, and resetting network settings, but none restored cellular connectivity. The user also reported using a dual-SIM configuration with two different carriers, with neither connection functioning, which suggests the issue was not related to a carrier outage or network-specific disruption.<br/>
<br/>
Baseband hardware failures are uncommon in modern smartphones due to extensive factory testing and validation processes, but large-scale production inevitably includes a small percentage of defective units. Apple has historically replaced and collected devices that exhibit unusual behavior for internal analysis, particularly when new technologies are involved. At present, there is no evidence that the issue is widespread or indicative of a broader reliability concern. <br/>
<br/>
As Apple prepares for the next generation of iPhones, real-world reliability data from early deployments of the C1 and C1X modems is likely to be closely monitored internally as part of the company's ongoing development work.
